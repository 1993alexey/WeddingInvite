Our Story

“Happiness is only real when shared” ― Jon Krakauer

  • How we met (Alex)

    April 10, 2018

    While browsing the internet, I came across a job post that caught my attention. BYU-Idaho was looking for a Web-Developer at the technology department, so I submitted my resume and went to bed. Within a few days, I was contacted by a BYUI recruiter and invited for an interview that followed by a coding challenge. As I was working on the assigned task, I was completely ignorant of what was happening around me – people talking, phones ringing, and a copier machine making the “dzj-j-j-j-j-j” sound.

    I leaned back, took a deep breath, and was about to return to my coding-zombie mode when I noticed a beautiful girl who was sitting two chairs away from me. She was wearing a blue floral blouse, a black skirt, and very red lipstick. “Wow, man… just look at her. She’s so adorable!” I think she is also a new hired employee like me. A voice echoed in my mind. I gather my courage and said, “Hey, would you like to sit next to me? We can help each other out.” She looked at me and said: “Sure!”

  • Should I Be Brave Again? (Lu)

    September 12, 2018

    Even though we were working in the same department and interacting with each other almost every day, we were just friends for about five month. I began to wonder if he could possibly be A one. One day while I was working, a professor at BYU-Idaho came to me for help with his online course problems. After helping him, he asked me: “Have you found your eternal companion?” While I was thinking about how to answer this unusual question, the professor turned to Alex and asked: “Are you married? Will you go out with this girl?” After Alex said yes, my cheeks blushed. Then the professor asked me the same question again. My heart almost jumped out of my chest when all my co-worker stared at us and wondered what just happend.

  • First Date (Alex)

    September 15, 2018

    “If you want to get a girl, make the first date memorable,” I was thinking to myself while walking home from work. I’ve had many first dates before with different outcomes: some of them turned out well, others didn’t; however, I was determined to take her on a date that she wouldn’t be able to forget.

    I went to a local store to buy matches, firewood, hot dog buns, and hot dog sausages. Then I picked her up at about 9 PM, and we drove to an open area known as “The Sand Dunes” 20 miles away from Rexburg. After starting the fire, we laid down on a blanket, looked at the stars, and made wishes. The sky was clear and we could clearly see the Milky Way galaxy. The mood was set, and I felt like it was a perfect time for the first kiss…

  • Engagement (Lu)

    January 12, 2019

    Dating Alex was an adventure. Our personalities are so similar but also very different in some ways. At first, I had so much fear because of the past dating experiences. I feared to fail again; feared to love someone; feared to lose him. However, I learned that the greatest joy reqired faith, sacrifice, and love. I promised Heavenly Father I will once give all I can to pursue the greatest joy in life... I made a choice even before dating: Alex was the kind of person with whom I wanted to spend every second of my life.

    He didn’t propose until after we dated for 3 months. I was clueless as to when he was going to propose. One day, he picked me up from home, and we went to a store to buy something (I don’t remember what it was). As we were passing the Porter Park, he suddenly parked, got out of the car, and invited me to follow him. The weather was horrible. Deep snow and strong wind made me want to go back to the car. As we got closer to the middle of the park, he grabbed my hand, looked into my eyes, stood on one knee, took out a ring, and said: “If you marry me, I will make you the happiest women in the world.” The blood rushed through my body. I completely forgot about the cold weather and the snow in my shoes. I knew I wanted to marry him, but instead of saying “YES,” I decided to play a tough cookie. After gathering my thoughts, I said: “If you promise to do all the dishes in the house, I will marry you.” Without hesitation, he promised to do the dishes, then lifted me up, and carried back to the car.
